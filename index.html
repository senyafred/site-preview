Frederick Senya

PhD Candidate in Applied Mathematics · Mathematical & Computational Biology · University of Vermont

“Modeling the world, teaching the why.”

fsenya@uvm.edu

I am an applied mathematician working at the intersection of mathematics, biology, and computation. I build and analyze mathematical models of living systems — turning differential equations, optimization, and data into insight about how biological systems behave in space and time.

I am a PhD candidate in Applied Mathematics at the University of Vermont, advised by Prof. Daniel Bentil. Recently I developed an equation-based framework that couples flux balance analysis with reaction–diffusion PDEs to simulate the spatio-temporal dynamics of microbial communities. I also work on predictive modeling of chronic-disease progression, Bayesian inference, and complex-systems models. I have a broad set of interests, including:

  • Mathematical & Computational Biology
  • Systems Biology & Flux Balance Analysis
  • Complex Systems & Agent-Based Modeling
  • Numerical Methods & Scientific Computing
  • Bayesian Inference & Data Science
  • Biogeochemical Modeling
  • Differential Equations (ODEs / PDEs)
  • Dynamical Systems & Stability Analysis

I am currently open to research and data-science opportunities. Feel free to get in touch, explore my research, or see my teaching.
