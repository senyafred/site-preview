Research

My research develops and analyzes mathematical models of biological and physical systems, spanning genome-scale metabolism, disease transmission, and complex collective behavior.

Spatial metabolic modeling — FBA meets reaction–diffusion

I developed an equation-based framework that integrates flux balance analysis (FBA) with reaction–diffusion PDEs to capture how microbial communities grow and interact across space and time. A central application studies oxygen-driven reductive stress in Pseudomonas aeruginosa biofilms, quantifying stress as a metabolic flux. This work is the basis of my first-author preprint (Senya et al., 2026).

Disease transmission modeling

I build ODE and PDE models of infectious-disease dynamics — including reaction–diffusion and chemotaxis models of schistosomiasis transmission with 2D spatial simulation — and compartmental epidemic models such as SVEIR with vaccination.

Predictive modeling for health

Working with virtual-care data, I applied predictive modeling to classify chronic kidney disease patients by their probability of stage degeneration from longitudinal eGFR trajectories. This co-authored work was presented at the SIAM Mathematical Problems in Industry (MPI) workshop.

Bayesian inference & complex systems

I use Bayesian hierarchical modeling and MCMC to quantify uncertainty in experimental data (including pupillometry / psycholinguistics), and I study complex systems through agent-based models such as the Vicsek model of collective motion and stochastic cellular automata.

Code for much of this work is on GitHub — see Projects.
